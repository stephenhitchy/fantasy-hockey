STANDARD PLAYOFF BLUEPRINT

CORE BEHAVIOR

- Standings lock into playoff seeds when the postseason begins.
- One complete six-game fantasy cycle equals one playoff round.
- The bracket is fixed after seeding; it does not reseed between rounds.
- A tied playoff matchup advances the higher seed.
- Regular-season wins, losses, ties, Points For, and Points Against freeze once
  the playoffs begin.
- Teams outside the championship field continue in consolation/classification
  games so every league team receives a final placement.
- Existing roster, IR, waiver, injury, projection, and cycle-scoring systems
  remain active and unchanged.

FORMAT BY LEAGUE SIZE

2-3 teams
- 2 championship qualifiers
- 12 regular-season cycles
- 1 playoff cycle: League Championship

4-7 teams
- 4 championship qualifiers
- 11 regular-season cycles
- 2 playoff cycles: Semifinals and League Championship
- Seeds 1 vs 4 and 2 vs 3

8-12 teams
- 6 championship qualifiers
- 10 regular-season cycles
- 3 playoff cycles: Opening Round, Semifinals, League Championship
- Seeds 1 and 2 receive first-round byes
- Opening Round: 3 vs 6 and 4 vs 5
- Semifinals: Seed 1 faces the 4/5 winner; Seed 2 faces the 3/6 winner

SEEDING ORDER

1. Win percentage, with a tie counting as half a win
2. Points For
3. Point differential
4. Total wins
5. Team name and owner ID as stable final fallbacks

SEASON LENGTH

The app reserves exactly 13 complete fantasy matchup periods, representing the
first 78 scheduled NHL games for each NHL team. The number of regular-season
cycles automatically shortens to make room for the appropriate playoff format.

FIRESTORE STORAGE

/leagues/{leagueId}/playoffs/current

The document stores frozen seeds, bracket matchups, round state, scores,
winners, tie-break flags, champion, runner-up, third place, and all final
placements.
