SPLIT DRAFT & CYCLE PROJECTIONS V1 — METHODOLOGY

1. TWO DIFFERENT QUESTIONS

Season Draft Projection asks:
"How valuable should this player be over a full season?"

Next Cycle Projection asks:
"How many fantasy points should this player produce in the upcoming six-team-
game cycle, given form, role, schedule position, and availability?"

The Draft Room and Auto-Draft use Season Draft Rank. Free Agents defaults to
Next Cycle Rank.

2. STABLE SEASON DRAFT OUTLOOK

During the offseason, the historical baseline is:

- 70% most recently completed season
- 20% second-most-recent completed season
- 10% conservative positional baseline

Once the current season begins, current-season performance gradually receives
up to 65% weight as the sample approaches 82 games. The remaining weight stays
distributed across the historical 70/20/10 foundation.

The completed season pace is still capped by sample size and positional bounds,
so a small or incomplete sample cannot produce an extreme projection.

Draft-only trend adjustments:

- Last 20 appearances only
- Maximum recent-trend movement: plus or minus 3%
- Maximum recent role/TOI movement: plus or minus 3%
- Last 3 and Last 5 are display-only

The draft reliability penalty is deliberately smaller than the next-cycle
penalty so a short volatile stretch does not erase established season value.

3. SHARED SEASON DRAFT SCORE

For every position, replacement level uses the league's configured maxTeams,
not merely the number of managers who happen to have joined at refresh time.

Season Draft Score is:

- 75% stable talent score
- 25% positional scarcity/value score

Skaters share one talent scale. Team goalie units have their own raw scoring
scale and receive a modest comparison adjustment so their larger team-unit
point totals do not automatically occupy every top overall slot.

Deterministic tie-breakers use projection, reliability, name, and asset key.
Every manager therefore receives the same order.

4. NEXT-CYCLE PROJECTION

The next-cycle baseline primarily follows current-season scoring pace once
current games exist. It regresses toward the previous season until the current
sample becomes trustworthy.

Recent form uses:

- Last 10 appearances: 65%
- Last 20 appearances: 35%

Maximum next-cycle form movement:

- plus or minus 6% when current-season data exists
- plus or minus 4% when only prior-season form is available

Recent role/TOI movement is capped at plus or minus 4%.

5. MISSED GAMES AND SHORT INJURY APPEARANCES

A true zero-point appearance counts as a zero.

A final NHL team game in which the skater did not appear is tracked as a missed
team game and is excluded from recent performance averages. It does not become
a fake zero-point game.

A game with unusually low TOI compared with the player's normal workload
receives partial weight. This limits the damage from a game in which the player
left early because of injury.

6. UPCOMING AVAILABILITY

Healthy Cycle is the player's expected production if available for every game.

Expected Games uses the shared injury status, upcoming NHL team game dates, and
an external return date when available.

Next Cycle is:

Healthy per-game expectation × expected games available

Active players normally receive the full scheduled game count. Day-to-Day,
Out, IR, LTIR, Suspended, Personal Leave, and Unknown statuses adjust expected
appearances and reliability without permanently lowering the healthy skill
baseline.

7. SNAPSHOT TIMING

A Version 2 snapshot contains both Season Draft Rank and Next Cycle Rank.

- Draft Setup creates an initial snapshot when a scheduled draft is saved.
- The commissioner browser refreshes the injury report and final snapshot 20
  minutes before the scheduled start.
- The final snapshot remains draft-fresh for 45 minutes.
- The pointer is published only after every asset document has been saved.
- Live draft clients reload the frozen pointer instead of calculating locally.

8. INTENDED DIFFERENCES FROM PUBLIC RANKINGS

The app does not hardcode a public top-40 list. Its custom scoring includes
hits, blocks, time on ice, diminishing goals and assists, and team goalie units.
Some differences are expected.

The stable board should nevertheless keep established elite season-long players
near the top and prevent a five- or ten-game hot/cold streak from moving a
player by a dozen or more overall spots without stronger evidence.
